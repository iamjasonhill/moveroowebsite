# Moveroo Brand Writing Guide

Use this file as the repo-level source of truth for website copy, prose, section messaging, and CTA language.
When a writing skill or drafting workflow is used in this repo, prefer the guidance here over generic defaults.

## Brand Voice

Moveroo should sound:

- clear
- capable
- practical
- calm
- warm without sounding soft
- Australian without forcing slang
- simple
- clean

Moveroo should not sound:

- like a generic SaaS template
- overly clever
- pretentious
- salesy for the sake of it
- padded with filler
- internal or meta

## Writing Standards

Default to simple language.
If a shorter, plainer word works, use it.

Good grammar matters.
Sentences should be clean, correctly punctuated, and easy to read on the first pass.

Prefer:

- short to medium sentence length
- plain English
- direct sentence structure
- strong nouns and verbs

Avoid:

- inflated wording
- decorative business language
- over-explaining simple ideas
- sounding polished at the expense of clarity

## Tone By Page Type

### Homepage

Job:

- sell the overall Moveroo promise
- show that we coordinate more than one type of move
- build trust quickly

Tone:

- strongest brand energy in the site
- confident and transport-led
- concise, not essay-like
- simple before stylish

### Services

Job:

- help people choose the right service path
- classify needs clearly
- reduce confusion between households, vehicles, storage, and specialty work

Tone:

- more functional than homepage
- decision-led, not narrative-led
- specific about who each path is for
- plain and easy to scan

### Moving Cars

Job:

- act as the vehicle-transport lane
- speak to people whose main requirement is moving a vehicle
- support related logistics without becoming a generic merger page

Tone:

- transport-specific
- direct
- trustworthy
- simple and matter-of-fact

### Contact

Job:

- reduce friction
- make the next step obvious
- reassure people that they will reach the right team

Tone:

- shortest and clearest prose on the site
- action-led
- supportive
- plain and friction-free

### Legal And Support Pages

Job:

- provide clarity
- build trust through structure and readability
- feel like part of the same site without reading like marketing copy

Tone:

- restrained
- calm
- plain-language first
- formal enough to feel reliable, but never stiff

## Messaging Priorities

Lead with:

- what is being moved
- who the service is for
- what Moveroo coordinates
- what happens next

Do not lead with:

- abstract claims
- vague quality words
- commentary about the page itself
- clever phrasing that makes the line harder to understand

## Preferred Language

Prefer phrases like:

- coordinated move
- vehicle transport
- household move
- specialty freight
- move brief
- quote path
- callback
- wider move
- support lane
- coordination team

Use verbs like:

- move
- coordinate
- transport
- arrange
- collect
- deliver
- book
- plan

## Avoid

Avoid phrases like:

- seamless solutions
- end-to-end excellence
- innovative platform
- streamlined experience
- premium service provider
- we understand that every move is unique
- this page is for
- a transport-specific page for

Avoid these habits:

- repeating the headline in softer words
- describing the content instead of helping the reader
- making services and homepage sound interchangeable
- writing long support paragraphs when two short lines would do
- using fancy wording where normal wording is stronger
- letting grammar slip in the name of sounding casual

## CTA Rules

Primary CTAs should describe a real next action.

Prefer:

- Start your quote
- Book a callback
- Talk to our team
- See the service path
- Start a vehicle quote

Avoid vague CTAs unless context already makes the action obvious.

## Structure Rules

For most marketing sections:

- one clear headline
- one support paragraph or two short sentences
- optional proof points
- one primary CTA

If a section starts feeling crowded:

- cut repetition first
- split mixed ideas second
- only add more copy if the user genuinely needs it to decide

## Differentiation Rules

If two pages start sounding too similar:

- make the homepage broader and more brand-led
- make services more classificatory
- make moving-cars more vehicle-led
- make contact more action-led
- make legal pages quieter and more utility-led

Do not fix overlap by swapping adjectives.
Change the job of the copy.

## Quick Examples

Weak:

- A transport-specific page for vehicle moves, not a generic merger notice.

Stronger:

- Use this lane when the vehicle is the main thing being moved.

Weak:

- We provide seamless moving solutions for homes, vehicles, storage, and more.

Stronger:

- Coordinate household moves, vehicle transport, storage, and specialty freight through one team.

Weak:

- Learn more

Stronger:

- Start your quote

## Final Check

Before publishing new copy, ask:

- Does this sound like Moveroo rather than a generic removals site?
- Is the language simple and clean?
- Is the page doing one main job clearly?
- Did we choose concrete nouns and verbs?
- Is the grammar correct and the punctuation tidy?
- Is the CTA specific?
- Would this still read cleanly on mobile?
